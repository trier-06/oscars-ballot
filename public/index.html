Ten Years of Changing Hollywood · 2015 – 2026

Ten Years of #OscarsSoWhite

A Tweet That Changed Hollywood

On January 15, 2015, April Reign posted a tweet that would redefine the conversation about diversity in film. The 87th Academy Awards had just announced nominations without a single person of color in any of the 20 acting slots. Her response was three words: #OscarsSoWhite they asked to touch my hair.

What started as a moment of Black Twitter brilliance became a global movement. The following year, when the 88th Oscars nominations were equally whitewashed, the hashtag erupted again — this time reaching every corner of the internet, every major newsroom, and the desks of Academy leadership.

The Academy's response was unprecedented: they doubled their membership with a commitment to diversifying who gets to vote. New representation standards — the A through Z Diversity Standards — were enacted for Best Picture eligibility. Studios reconsidered what stories they greenlit, who they cast, and who they hired behind the camera. Ten years later, the Academy's membership has transformed. And in 2026, we are seeing the results.

"#OscarsSoWhite has always encompassed all traditionally underrepresented communities — not just race and ethnicity. It's also gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, First Nations status, and age."

— April Reign, creator of #OscarsSoWhite

Why 2026 Is Historic

A Record-Breaking Year

The 98th Oscars represent the most diverse nominations in the Academy's history. This is the second year under the new diversity rules enacted directly in response to #OscarsSoWhite. The numbers speak for themselves.

🎬
Sinners broke the all-time Oscar nomination record with 16 nominations — surpassing Titanic and La La Land.
All-time record: most nominated film in Oscar history
✊
10 of those 16 Sinners nominations went to Black artists — tying the all-time record set by Judas and the Black Messiah (2021).
Tied all-time record for Black artist nominations in one film
🎥
Ryan Coogler is only the 7th Black filmmaker ever nominated for Best Director in the Academy's 98-year history.
No Black filmmaker has ever won Best Director
👗
Ruth E. Carter (Costume Design, Sinners) earned her 5th nomination — making her the most-nominated Black woman in Oscar history, surpassing Viola Davis.
Most-nominated Black woman in Oscar history
📷
Autumn Durald Arkapaw (Cinematography, Sinners) is the first woman of color, first Filipino woman, and only the 4th woman ever nominated for Best Cinematography in 98 years. She also shot the first IMAX film by a woman.
First woman of color nominated for Best Cinematography
🏗️
Hannah Beachler (Production Design, Sinners) earned her 2nd nomination — she remains the only Black woman ever nominated in that category.
Only Black woman ever nominated for Best Production Design
🎞️
Zinzi Coogler is the first Filipina producer and 3rd Black woman nominated for Best Picture. She and Ryan are the first Black married couple nominated together in that category.
First Black married couple nominated for Best Picture together
🌎
Wagner Moura is the first Brazilian-born actor ever nominated for Best Actor in the Academy's 98-year history.
First Brazilian-born Best Actor nominee in Oscar history
🇵🇷
Benicio Del Toro (Puerto Rican) earned his 3rd Oscar nomination, continuing his legacy as one of the most acclaimed Latino actors in Hollywood history.
3rd nomination for a Puerto Rican icon
🔊
The Sirât sound team (Amanda Villavieja, Laia Casanovas, Yasmina Praderas) is the first all-female team ever nominated for Best Sound in Oscar history.
First all-female sound nomination in 98 years
✈️
Sev Ohanian (Producer, Sinners) is the first Armenian American nominated for Best Picture.
First Armenian American Best Picture nominee
🎞️
Kaouther Ben Hania (The Voice of Hind Rajab) is the first Arab woman to direct three Oscar-nominated films. If she wins, she will be the first Arab woman to receive an Academy Award.
First Arab woman to direct 3 Oscar-nominated films
🇵🇸
Palestinian actor Motaz Malhees was barred from attending the ceremony due to the US travel ban. His statement: "You can block a passport. You cannot block a voice."
Nominated, but barred from attending his own ceremony
♀
A record 74 women nominated total — an all-time high for the Academy Awards.
All-time record: most women nominated in a single year
🌮
Yvett Merino is the first Latina nominated in animation at the Academy Awards.
First Latina nomination in animated feature
🏳️‍🌈
LGBTQ+ representation includes A Friend of Dorothy, Come See Me in the Good Light, and Elio — small but visible progress in an area that remains underrepresented.
LGBTQIA+ stories still fighting for space

"You can block a passport. You cannot block a voice."

— Motaz Malhees, Palestinian actor, The Voice of Hind Rajab · barred from attending the Oscars by US travel ban
